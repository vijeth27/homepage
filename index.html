About me

I am currently a doctoral candidate in the Aerospace Engineering Department at University of Illinois-Urbana Champaign. I am advised by Prof. Cedric Langbort. I am currently a research assistant at Coordinated Science Laboratory. I completed my undergraduate education in the Aerospace Engineering Department at IIT Bombay.

Research Overview

My research focuses on information design in strategic systems, where agents make decisions based on information provided by others. I study how information can be structured to influence behavior in settings such as electricity markets, human-robot interaction, and misinformation environments.

A central theme of my work is developing learning algorithms for signaling problems where the sender does not fully know how receivers interpret information. By combining tools from game theory, online learning, and control theory, my research aims to design communication strategies that remain effective even when agents learn, adapt, and respond strategically.

Research Themes

Applications of Information Design
I apply the information design framework to model strategic interactions in real-world domains such as electricity markets, human-robot interaction, and misinformation ecosystems. These applications demonstrate how strategically structuring information can improve system outcomes while highlighting the practical and computational challenges that arise when deploying such mechanisms.

Learning to Strategically Share Information
In many environments the sender does not know how receivers interpret information. My work develops learning algorithms that allow the sender to infer receiver responses and adapt communication strategies over time, even when receivers form beliefs based on past behavior or reputation.

Online Linear Control
I study online control problems where a controller must operate in the presence of adversarial disturbances and evolving system dynamics. My work proposes new regret benchmarks and efficient algorithms that improve performance over existing methods while providing stronger theoretical guarantees.
